Explore Athens

The Sights

Ancient wonders, neighborhoods & museums
Must-See Athens
The Ancient City
Acropolis & Parthenon
Ancient Hill · ~2–3 hrs · €30 (or combo ticket)
The crown jewel. The Parthenon, Erechtheion (with Caryatid porch), Temple of Athena Nike, and the Propylaea gateway. No straight lines — subtle curves were engineered to correct for optical distortion.
⚡ Book the 8 AM slot — cooler, no crowds, cruise ships haven't docked yet. In July, midday is brutal and the site may close 1–5 PM in extreme heat (>40°C).
🎭 On the south-slope walk-up, pause at the Theatre of Dionysus — the birthplace of Western theatre, where the premieres of Sophocles, Euripides and Aristophanes were staged. It's included on your Acropolis ticket and most visitors breeze right past it on the way to the Odeon. Zero extra cost or detour.
Must-Do Book Ahead Hot at Midday
Acropolis Museum
South Slope · ~2–3 hrs · €20
World-class museum built above an excavated ancient neighborhood (visible through glass floors). The top-floor Parthenon Gallery is oriented to mirror the Parthenon itself and displays the original frieze alongside casts of the British Museum pieces — a deliberate statement.
⚡ Save for 1–3 PM — fully air-conditioned, perfect midday refuge. Rooftop restaurant has direct Parthenon views.
Must-Do A/C Refuge
Ancient Agora & Temple of Hephaestus
Monastiraki · ~1.5–2 hrs · included in Acropolis combo
The civic heart of ancient Athens — Socrates walked here. The Temple of Hephaestus is the best-preserved ancient Greek temple in existence (survived because it was converted to a church). The reconstructed Stoa of Attalos houses an excellent museum.
⚡ Visit 8–10 AM for shade and cool air. Stoa museum is air-conditioned. Monastiraki flea market is right next door.
🔮 Inside the Stoa of Attalos museum, hunt down the Magic Sphere of Helios — a small marble orb carved with the sun god and cryptic ritual symbols, thought to have been used in ancient Greek magic. An Atlas Obscura favorite that's easy to overlook among the bigger pieces.
Must-Do Combo Ticket
Temple of Olympian Zeus
Near Syntagma · ~30–45 min · included in Acropolis combo
Once the largest temple in the ancient world. Construction started 6th century BC, finished 638 years later. Only 15 of 104 original Corinthian columns remain — but they are 17 meters tall. Scale hits differently in person. Hadrian's Arch stands nearby.
Combo Ticket Quick Stop
Roman Agora & Tower of the Winds
Monastiraki/Plaka · ~1 hr · included in Acropolis combo
The Tower of the Winds (Horologion) is an octagonal marble clocktower built ~50 BC — simultaneously a sundial, weather vane, and water clock. Arguably the world's oldest surviving meteorological station. Each face depicts a wind deity in relief.
Combo Ticket Underrated
Panathenaic Stadium (Kallimarmaro)
Near Kolonaki · ~45–60 min · ~€10
The only stadium in the world built entirely of marble. Site of the first modern Olympic Games in 1896. You can run a lap on the same track.
⚡ Early morning light is best; marble is blinding at midday.
Historic
Neighborhoods & Squares
Monastiraki Square & Flea Market
Central Athens · free
Athens' most chaotic and energetic crossroads. Ancient ruins, a Byzantine mosque, flea market stalls, street food, and legendary rooftop bars with Acropolis backdrop — all in one square. Flea market sprawls south on Ifestou Street.
⚡ A for Athens and 360 Cocktail Bar rooftop bars at sunset (8–9 PM) — Acropolis in golden hour light. Non-negotiable.
Free Sunset Essential
Plaka Neighborhood
Below the Acropolis · free · half-day+
Oldest inhabited neighborhood in Athens. Pedestrian-only labyrinth of neoclassical mansions, bougainvillea stairways, and tavernas. Touristy on the main streets — go one block off the obvious path and it becomes genuinely lovely.
Free Romantic
Syntagma Square — Changing of the Guard
City Center · free · 30 min
Evzones (Presidential Guard) in traditional uniform guard the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier 24/7. Guard changes every hour. The Sunday 11 AM ceremony includes a full marching band and the complete regiment.
⚡ The full Sunday grand ceremony (11 AM) only happens on Jul 5 — your departure day — so you'll likely miss it. Catch one of the hourly changes earlier in the trip instead.
Free Cultural
Museums & Culture
National Archaeological Museum
North of Syntagma · 2–3+ hrs · €20
The most important Greek antiquities collection in the world. Mask of Agamemnon, the Antikythera Mechanism (world's first analog computer, ~100 BC), the Artemision Bronze (Zeus or Poseidon), and the Thira frescoes from ancient Santorini.
⚡ Wildly underrated relative to the Acropolis. Less crowded, fully A/C, and the collection is extraordinary. A strong midday option.
📷 Brett: non-flash photography is permitted in all galleries (unlike the Acropolis Museum). Arrive at 8 AM opening to shoot the icons before the tour groups, and bring a fast prime (f/1.8–2.8) for the dim bronze halls.
🔭 Don't walk past the Antikythera Mechanism (Atlas Obscura's #1 Athens pick) — a 2,000-year-old geared bronze "computer" that modeled the movements of the sun, moon and planets. It's a small, easy-to-miss case but one of the most astonishing objects in the building.
Underrated A/C Refuge Photo Spot
B&E Goulandris Foundation
Pangrati · 1.5–2 hrs · ~€10
A refurbished neoclassical building housing an extraordinary private modern-art collection — Van Gogh, Picasso, Rodin, Giacometti, and Degas' "Little Dancer Aged Fourteen." A quiet, civilized counterpoint to the ancient ruins, in the up-and-coming Pangrati neighborhood near the Panathenaic Stadium.
⚡ The lush garden café-restaurant (by the Deipnosofistirion team) is a perfect Mediterranean lunch after the galleries. Pair it with Louvron kafeneio right across the street.
Underrated A/C Refuge Garden Café
Odeon of Herodes Atticus — Live Performance
South slope Acropolis · 2–3 hrs · €15–100+
Roman-era stone theater built 161 AD, still in use. Athens & Epidaurus Festival (June–August) stages concerts, ballet, and theater here. Illuminated Parthenon behind the stage. 5,000 seats. One of those experiences that is almost too much.
⚡ Check greekfestival.gr now and book tickets — peak summer slots sell out. Affordable tickets exist (€15–30).
Book Ahead Bucket List
Lycabettus Hill
Kolonaki · 1–1.5 hrs · free (walk) or €7 funicular
Highest point in Athens (277 m). Chapel of Saint George at the top. Panoramic views to the sea, the Acropolis, and on clear days, the island of Aegina.
⚡ Arrive 30–45 min before sunset — one of Athens' great golden-hour views.

Off the Beaten Path
Hidden Neighborhoods
Anafiotika
Above Plaka · free
Whitewashed cubic houses, blue doors, bougainvillea — a Cycladic island village teleported onto the Acropolis hillside. Built in the 1800s by workers from the island of Anafi who constructed their homes in their homeland style. Two tiny rock churches embedded into the cliff. Most tourists walk right past the stairs.
⚡ Enter from upper Plaka lanes near Stratonos Street. Best in early morning or golden hour.
Hidden Photogenic Free
Strefi Hill — The Local's Lycabettus
Exarchia · free · no tourists
A small pine-covered hill in Exarchia that most travelers skip. Excellent panoramic views — Lycabettus in the foreground, Acropolis beyond — from a completely local setting. Basketball court, stray cats, Athenian families on evening walks. No funicular, no ticket booth.
⚡ Enter from Kallidromiou Street in Exarchia. Best at golden hour. Exarchia has a famously independent character — fine in daylight.
Free Local Vibe Sunset
Ancient Sites (No Crowds)
The Pnyx — World's First Parliament
West of Acropolis · free
A rocky hillside where the Ekklesia (Assembly of Athenian citizens) met from 507 BC onward — the world's first democratic parliament. The Bema (speaker's platform) is a rock-cut podium from which Themistocles, Pericles, and Demosthenes addressed citizens. You can stand on the exact spot.
⚡ Almost no one visits. Zero crowds. Free entry. You can stand where democracy was literally invented. Also has one of the best angles on the Acropolis in the city.
Free No Crowds Historic
Agios Dimitrios Loumbardiaris
Filopappou Hill · free
A serene 16th-century wooden-roofed Byzantine church on the wooded path up Filopappou Hill — right beside the Pnyx. "Loumbardiaris" means "the Bombardier": legend says St. Demetrius miraculously destroyed an Ottoman cannon (loumbarda) aimed at the church from the Acropolis. The 1950s renovation by architect Dimitris Pikionis — the same hand behind the beautiful hand-laid stone walkways all over the hill — is a quiet masterpiece of modern Greek design.
⚡ Pair it with the Pnyx in the same visit — they're a two-minute walk apart. The Pikionis stone path leading to it is half the reason to come. Cool, shaded, and almost always empty.
Free No Crowds Byzantine
Kerameikos Cemetery & Museum
NW of Agora · included in Acropolis combo
Athens' most important ancient cemetery, 12th century BC onward. The Street of Tombs is a preserved ancient road lined with elaborate grave monuments (sculpted marble bulls, serpents, warriors). The Dipylon Gate — the largest ancient city gate in Greece. Cats live in the ruins.
⚡ Consistently one of the least-visited downtown sites. A tranquil, genuinely moving experience. Combine with Psirri neighborhood next door.
Combo Ticket No Crowds
First Cemetery of Athens
Near Panathenaic Stadium · free · ~1 hr
One of the most hauntingly beautiful and under-visited sites in Athens — the resting place of Greece's elite since the 1830s, and effectively a sprawling, pine-shaded outdoor museum of 19th-century neoclassical sculpture: elaborate marble mausoleums, weeping angels, and sphinxes. The most famous work is the "Sleeping Girl" (Koimomeni) by Yannoulis Chalepas.
📷 Brett: late-afternoon light filtering through the dark pine canopy onto whitewashed marble is magical. A 70–200mm isolates sculptural detail against the trees. Silence and respect for ongoing ceremonies are expected; photography is permitted.
Free No Crowds Photo Spot
Hidden Bars & Distilleries
TAF — The Art Foundation
Normanou 5 · cocktail bar + gallery · 10 AM–3 AM
Hidden behind an inconspicuous door 30 seconds from the Ancient Agora. An 1870s neoclassical building with a tree-filled courtyard that becomes a cocktail bar. Rotating contemporary art exhibitions upstairs. Locals drinking excellent cocktails under string lights. Virtually no tourists despite central location.
⚡ Duck into the dark entrance at Normanou 5 — the courtyard opens beyond. You'd walk past it a dozen times without knowing.
Hidden Local Vibe Art
📍 Normanou 5, Monastiraki
Brettos Distillery
Kydathineon 41, Plaka · open afternoons/evenings
Family-run distillery operating since 1909. The interior is lined floor-to-ceiling with hundreds of illuminated colored glass bottles. They distill their own ouzo, tsipouro, rakomelo (warm raki with honey and cinnamon), brandy, and metaxa. Sample at the bar for a few euros per shot.
⚡ Bottles make excellent gifts — cheaper here than at the airport. The colored bottle wall alone is worth stepping inside.

Museums Worth Knowing
Benaki Museum of Islamic Art
Agion Asomaton 22, Kerameikos · World-class, empty
One of the top ten Islamic art collections in the world — 8,000+ works across ceramics, metalwork, textiles, and armor from the 8th through 19th centuries. The crown exhibit: a fully intact 17th-century Cairo mansion reception room with original marble interior, transplanted here.
⚡ Almost nobody visits. A walk from Thisio Metro. Genuinely extraordinary and consistently uncrowded.
Underrated No Crowds
Numismatic Museum (Schliemann's Mansion)
Panepistimiou 12, near Syntagma · cheap
One of the world's most important coin collections — 500,000+ coins from 1400 BC to modern era. But the real draw is the building: the mansion of Heinrich Schliemann (the archaeologist who excavated Troy and Mycenae). Pompeian frescoes, floor mosaics of Trojan finds, Homer inscriptions on the walls.
⚡ Even if coins bore you, the interior of this mansion is stunning. Walk along the adjacent neoclassical "Athenian Trilogy" — University, National Library, Academy of Athens.
Underrated Historic Building
Old-Athens Tavernas & Cafés
Diporto
Sokratous 9, Central Market · cash only · lunch–early evening
A ~130-year-old basement taverna with no sign, no menu, and no decor to speak of — just two unmarked doors on a graffiti-splattered building near the Central Market. You sit at communal tables among market workers and old Athenians while the owner tells you what's cooking that day: chickpeas, baked fish, wine straight from the barrel. One of the last truly unchanged tavernas in the city.
⚡ Notoriously hard to find — look for the twin doors on the corner of Sokratous & Theatrou. Bring cash, go hungry, and don't expect English or a wine list. This is the real thing.
Hidden Cheap Eats Old Athens
Kafeneio Oraia Ellas
Mitropoleos 59, Monastiraki · café · day–evening
"Beautiful Greece" — a kafeneio serving strong Greek coffee since 1839, making it one of the oldest cafés in Athens. Once the haunt of the city's poets and intellectuals (Hans Christian Andersen sipped here), its rooms are crammed with antique bric-a-brac, and the upstairs terrace has a quiet Acropolis view above the bustle of Monastiraki.
⚡ Sits above the "Loom" (Pandrosou arcade) folk-art shop. Order a Greek coffee metrios (medium-sweet) and a loukoumi, and head up to the terrace mid-afternoon.

Day Trips & Escapes
Hydra Island
Catamaran from Piraeus · 1–1.75 hrs · €35–40 each way
The most photogenic island in the Saronic Gulf — a strict preservation order bans all motor vehicles, so everything moves by foot, wooden boat, or donkey. The main town rises like a grey-and-white stone amphitheater from the sea, full of fortified 18th-century sea-captain mansions, fishermen mending nets, and sun-drenched cats on whitewashed parapets.
📷 Brett's Jul 1 solo day. Morning ferries depart Piraeus 08:00–10:00; returns run to ~19:30. Book ahead in July high season. The car-free silence and Aegean light are made for black-and-white architectural shots.
Island Photo Day Book Ahead
Nafplio
KTEL bus from Kifissos · ~2 hr 10 min · €13–17
The first capital of modern Greece — a layered Venetian/Ottoman/neoclassical old town on the Peloponnese coast, with bougainvillea-choked alleys, marble squares, and the tiny island fortress of Bourtzi floating in the harbor. Above it all sits the massive Palamidi Fortress, reached by 999 stone steps for unrivaled panoramas of the Argolic Gulf.
📷 Brett's Jul 2 solo day. Buses leave roughly every 2 hrs (ideal 06:00 / 08:00 / 09:30); returns until ~20:00, leaving room for sunset shots. Crosses the Corinth Canal en route.
Photo Day Venetian Town
Vouliagmeni Lake
~20 km south · ~30–40 min taxi · ~€15 entry
A natural thermal lake where underground waters emerge into a brackish karst lake with overhanging stalactites. Water maintained at 22–29°C year-round, rich in minerals. Fish perform a natural exfoliating service. Used therapeutically since antiquity — Romans built baths here.
⚡ Perfect July escape from the city heat. Morning visit gets the best light through the stalactite formations.
Swimming July Essential
Kaisariani Monastery
~5 km east · 20 min taxi · free/cheap entry
An 11th-century Byzantine monastery built into forested slopes of Mount Hymettus. Main church covered in 16th–17th century frescoes. Around it: monks' cells, bathhouse, olive press, millhouse — all standing. Marked hiking trails to hidden chapels above. A spring has flowed here since antiquity; the ancient Athenians sacrificed here to Aphrodite.
⚡ Cool, quiet, forested — a complete escape from city heat barely a taxi ride away. Athenians hike here on weekends; tourists skip it entirely.
Escape Hiking No Crowds
Syntagma Metro Station — Archaeology Underground
Free — you're already here
During construction in the 1990s, workers excavated a significant section of the ancient city — finding thousands of artifacts including burial sites, a Roman bath, and an intact 4th-century BC skeleton. All displayed museum-style in glass cases throughout the station. The skeleton is visible from the platform.
⚡ You are waiting for your train next to an ancient Athenian. Free, on the way to everywhere. Just slow down and read the cases.
Free Hidden in Plain Sight
Getting Around Athens
Metro Lines Line 2 (Red) → Acropolis station · Line 3 (Blue) → Airport, Syntagma, Monastiraki · Line 1 (Green) → Piraeus port. Tap Visa/Mastercard/Apple Pay directly at validators (no ticket needed for city fares). Single fare: €1.20. Daily cap: €4.10. Airport: €9 one-way.
Taxis & Rideshare Use FreeNow or Uber apps — eliminates meter scams entirely. Official yellow taxis: insist on the meter before entering. Airport flat rate: €40 (5 AM–midnight), €55 (midnight–5 AM) — fixed by law.
Money & ATMs
ATM Warning Use only bank ATMs: Alpha Bank, Piraeus Bank, Eurobank, National Bank of Greece. Avoid Euronet and private ATMs — excessive fees.
Always Pay in Euros When any ATM or card terminal offers to charge in USD, ALWAYS choose "pay in EUR (local currency)." Dynamic Currency Conversion is a ripoff — every time.

July Heat — Critical
Average High: 95–105°F (35–40°C) Heatwaves can push above 42°C (108°F). The Acropolis may close automatically 1–5 PM when temps exceed 40°C. Book the 8 AM Acropolis slot — it's 5–10°C cooler and the crowds are a fraction of peak hours.
Daily Strategy 8 AM – 11 AM: outdoor sites · 11 AM – 4 PM: museums, cafes, A/C · 4–8 PM: outdoor again · 8 PM+: dinner, rooftops, evening walks. Athens locals dine at 9 PM or later.
Scams to Know
!
Taxi meter "broken"
Driver claims no meter, quotes inflated fare. Use FreeNow/Uber instead. Airport flat rate is €40 — fixed by law.
!
Fake bracelet "gift"
Stranger wraps bracelet on your wrist, then demands payment. Decline immediately and keep walking.
!
Restaurant bill inflation
Always ask to see the menu with prices before ordering. Review the bill item by item near major tourist sites.
!
Unordered table items
Bread, olives, flowers placed without ordering — then charged. Ask if anything is complimentary before eating it.
!
Bird dropping / yogurt scam
Fake substance lands on you, "helpful" stranger offers to clean it and pickpockets you. Decline help and step into a shop.
!
Euronet ATMs
Private ATMs with excessive fees. Stick to Greek bank ATMs (Alpha Bank, Piraeus, Eurobank, National Bank).
Dress Code at Religious Sites
Churches & Monasteries Shoulders and knees must be covered (both men and women). Women may be asked to cover their head — carry a scarf. Remove hats before entering any church. A lightweight cotton scarf doubles as a sun cover and church wrap.
